# Landing Page Redesign Proposal for Owner-Operators

## Target Audience
Owner-operators: financial advisors, consultants, small service businesses, e-commerce owners — anyone paying for a stack of SaaS tools that don't fully serve their needs.

## Current Problems with the Landing Page
1. Headline is about Rich, not their pain
2. Case study only shows e-commerce (LAR)
3. Differentiators describe what's built, not what they get
4. Assessment questions are e-commerce-centric
5. Stats bar is all e-commerce metrics
6. CTAs use developer jargon ("technical audit")
7. Process steps use dev language (CI/CD, architecture)

---

## HEADLINE (iterating)

Core insight to hit: They're not "spending" on software. They're WASTING money on subscriptions they haven't touched in months. The "we'll use it eventually" lie that costs $50-200/month per tool, compounding silently. And the fix isn't just reducing costs — it's eliminating them permanently. Custom software you own has no renewal date.

Direction:
- Hit the emotional pain of the recurring charge they can't justify
- The promise: not just savings, but ELIMINATION of the subscription treadmill
- Years from now, the software still works. Still $0/month.

Working candidates (needs refinement):
- "That subscription you haven't used in 3 months just renewed again."
- "You're wasting $X/month on software you barely use. What if you owned something that actually worked — and never charged you again?"
- "Stop renting software that does 60% of what you need."
- "The 'we'll use it eventually' subscription just cost you another $1,500 this year."

Rotating subtitles should reinforce:
- "That CRM you logged into twice last quarter? Still charging you."
- "12 subscriptions. 4 you actually use. All of them renewing next month."
- "What if your software cost $0/month — forever?"
- "Custom software that works for YOUR business. No monthly fees. No renewals. Ever."

## CASE STUDIES (repositioned)

### Case Study 1: Love Amethyst Rose (E-commerce)
**Before framing (pain-first):**
> An e-commerce owner paying for 8 different software subscriptions — website hosting, email marketing, ad tracking, inventory management, scheduling, analytics, payment processing, CRM. Her site still crashed on her biggest sales day. She was copying data between tools by hand. Nothing talked to each other.

**After framing (outcome-first):**
> We replaced the entire fragmented stack with one integrated system she owns outright. $30K revenue month. Zero downtime. Zero subscription fees for the tools we replaced. New products go live in 10 minutes instead of a full day. Marketing campaigns run on autopilot.

**The money line:** "She stopped renting 8 tools that did 60% of the job and started owning one system that does 100%."

### Case Study 2: Financial Advisor (Compliance + Cost)
**Before framing:**
> A financial advisor paying $200/month for Salesforce to track 50 leads. She used maybe 5% of its features. But she couldn't risk building something herself — SEC regulations mean a data breach doesn't just cost money, it can end her business. She was trapped: overpaying for a tool she barely used because compliance was the vendor's moat.

**After framing:**
> We built a custom CRM in 3 weeks — tailored to her exact workflow, SEC-compliant from the architecture up, and it costs $0/month to run. She owns every line of code and every byte of client data. No vendor can raise her price, change her terms, or hold her data hostage.

**The money line:** "Salesforce was charging her $2,400/year for a tool she used 5% of. Now she owns something that does 100% of what she needs — and will never send her another invoice."

## DIFFERENTIATORS (pain-first rewrite)

### Tab 1: "Stop paying for features you don't use"
You're subsidizing someone else's product roadmap. That enterprise CRM with 400 features? You use 12 of them. Those other 388 are why your bill keeps going up. We build exactly what you need — nothing more, nothing less. And it costs $0/month to keep.

### Tab 2: "Own it. Don't rent it."
SaaS companies love recurring revenue. You shouldn't. Custom software you own has no renewal date, no price increases, no "we're changing our terms of service" emails. Two years from now, it's still running. Still $0/month. Still yours.

### Tab 3: "AI without the risk"
45% of AI-generated code has security vulnerabilities (Veracode, 2025). You don't need to know what that means — you need to know someone who does. I use AI to build faster and cheaper, but every line is reviewed by a decade of professional engineering experience. Your data stays safe. Your compliance stays intact.

### Tab 4: "No more duct tape"
You've got Zapier connecting your email tool to your CRM to your spreadsheet to your calendar. 20 steps. $100/month. One break and the whole chain goes down. I replace duct-tape integrations with real systems that run reliably — for $6/month instead of $100.

## ASSESSMENT (rewritten for owner-operators)

### Questions:
1. "How many software subscriptions does your business pay for monthly?"
   - 1-3 / 4-8 / 9-15 / 16+

2. "How many of those do you actually use every week?"
   - All of them / Most / About half / Honestly, a few

3. "Have you ever kept paying for a subscription because canceling felt too risky?"
   - Yes, more than once / Once or twice / No / I don't track this closely enough to know

4. "Have you or your team tried using AI to build or automate something?"
   - Yes and it went well / Yes but it didn't work out / No but I'm curious / No and I'm skeptical

5. "If a vendor you rely on doubled their price tomorrow, what would you do?"
   - Panic — we're locked in / Scramble for alternatives / We'd be fine, we have options / We own our critical systems

### Results:
- **Low score:** "Your business is on a subscription treadmill. You're spending thousands per year on tools that don't fully work, and you're locked into vendors who know it. The good news: this is fixable."
- **Mid score:** "You've got some pieces in place, but you're still overpaying for underperforming software. A focused engagement could eliminate $500-1,500/month in waste."
- **High score:** "You're ahead of most. The next level is full ownership — replacing the remaining subscriptions with systems you control completely."

## PROCESS (plain language)

1. **Free Consultation** — "30 minutes. We look at what you're spending on software and what you're actually getting for it. No pitch. No commitment."
2. **Software Review** — "I map every tool you're paying for — what it costs, what it does, what it doesn't do, and what's trapping you. You get a clear picture of where your money goes."
3. **Build Phase** — "We replace the waste with software you own. Built with AI for speed, reviewed by a professional for safety. You see results in weeks."
4. **Handoff** — "You own everything. No monthly fees. No lock-in. No renewal notices. I'm there if you need me, gone if you don't."
