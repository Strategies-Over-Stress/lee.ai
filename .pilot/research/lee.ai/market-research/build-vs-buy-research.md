# Build vs Buy: Research Context for Owner-Operator Article

## 1. SaaS Subscription Fatigue

- **41% of consumers** report subscription fatigue (Readless, 2026)
- **42% of subscribers** kept paying for at least one subscription they stopped using
- SaaS costs per employee: **$8,700 (2024) → $9,100 (2025) → $10,800 projected (2026)** — steady annual increases
- Average company uses **130+ SaaS apps** and wastes **$18 billion collectively** on unused subscriptions (Zylo)
- Large orgs use **275+ SaaS apps** with overlapping functionality
- Organizations use only **47% of their SaaS licenses** — up to **$21M wasted per company annually**
- Small businesses spend **6-12% of revenue** on SaaS vs enterprises at 2-5%
- **SMBs spend more per employee** than larger enterprises due to less negotiating power
- Seat-based pricing declined from **21% to 15%** of SaaS market share in one year (Forrester, Sept 2025)

Sources:
- https://www.readless.app/blog/subscription-fatigue-statistics-2026
- https://zylo.com/blog/saas-statistics/
- https://colorlib.com/wp/saas-statistics/
- https://www.spendbase.co/spending-benchmarks/

## 2. AI Project Failure Rates

- **80.3% overall AI project failure rate** (RAND Corporation, 2025)
- **95% of generative AI pilots fail** — brittle workflows, misaligned expectations (MIT, 2025)
- Global enterprises invested **$684 billion in AI** in 2025; **$547 billion (80%+) failed** to deliver intended business value
- Large enterprises lose **average $7.2M per failed AI initiative**, abandoned **2.3 initiatives** in 2025
- **60% of AI projects** lacking AI-ready data will be abandoned through 2026 (Gartner)
- Industry failure rates: Financial services 82.1%, Healthcare 78.9%, Manufacturing 76.4%, Retail 73.8%
- Failure is almost never the model — it's **data readiness, workflow integration, and absence of defined outcomes**
- Success requires: clear pre-approval metrics (54% success vs 12% without), sustained sponsorship (68% vs 11%)

Sources:
- https://www.pertamapartners.com/insights/ai-project-failure-statistics-2026

## 3. AI-Generated Code Security Risks

- **45% of AI code samples failed security tests** — introduced OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities (Veracode, 2025)
- **62% of AI-generated code** contains design flaws or known security vulnerabilities
- **86% failed** to defend against cross-site scripting (CWE-80)
- **88% vulnerable** to log injection attacks (CWE-117)
- Critical vulnerabilities discovered in Claude Code itself allowing **remote code execution and API key theft** through malicious project configs (Check Point Research)
- A stolen API key gains **complete read/write access to all workspace files** + can exhaust API credits
- Three risk categories: insecure generated code, models vulnerable to attack, downstream feedback loops

Sources:
- https://www.veracode.com/blog/genai-code-security-report/
- https://research.checkpoint.com/2026/rce-and-api-token-exfiltration-through-claude-code-project-files-cve-2025-59536/

## 4. Custom Software vs SaaS Cost Economics

- Custom software development: **$100K-$400K** for medium projects, **$400K-$500K+** for enterprise
- SaaS: **$10-$300 per user/month** — cheaper upfront
- **Break-even at years 2-4** — faster with 20+ users, multiple SaaS tools, or >10% annual price increases
- **Tipping point: when monthly SaaS costs exceed $2,000-3,000**, custom becomes viable
- AI-assisted development ("vibe coding") has **fundamentally shifted the economics** — what cost $200K now costs a fraction
- Enterprises shifting away from building custom AI (2024 POCs mostly failed) toward commercial solutions — but the opposite is true for custom business apps built WITH AI

Sources:

- https://scandiweb.com/blog/why-custom-software-is-becoming-cheaper-than-saas
- https://codivox.com/blog/saas-vs-custom-software-2026/

## 5. SaaS Vendor Lock-in & Compliance

- Vendor lock-in creates **budget strain, compliance risk, and loss of leverage**
- Many agreements offer **little flexibility to scale down mid-term**
- Complex licensing rules **shift frequently** — vendors leverage audit threats to force unfavorable renewals
- Exit costs often hidden: data migration, proprietary formats, workflow disruption
- **10-25% "toxic spend"** on unused licenses
- EU Data Act (Sept 2025): requires switching rights with 2-month notice, no switching charges after Jan 2027, data portability in machine-readable formats
- Salesforce customers reducing seat counts by **10% due to AI efficiency** even as Salesforce maintains per-seat pricing

Sources:

- https://atonementlicensing.com/blog/saas-lock-in/
