## The AI Promise vs. The AI Reality

Every week there's a new AI tool promising you can "build an app in minutes." And technically, that's not wrong. You *can* get something working quickly.

But "working" and "ready for your business" are very different things.

Here's what the headlines don't tell you:

**[80-95% of AI projects fail](https://www.pertamapartners.com/insights/ai-project-failure-statistics-2026)** to deliver what they promised, according to RAND Corporation and MIT. Not because the AI isn't smart enough. Because nobody defined what "done" looked like before they started building.

**[45% of AI-written code has security holes](https://www.veracode.com/blog/genai-code-security-report/)** — the kind that can expose your customer data, your payment info, your private business records. AI optimizes for "it works," not "it's safe."

**Your private keys and passwords can end up places they shouldn't be.** In log files. In the AI provider's servers. In your codebase where anyone with access can see them. [Researchers recently found vulnerabilities](https://research.checkpoint.com/2026/rce-and-api-token-exfiltration-through-claude-code-project-files-cve-2025-59536/) that could let attackers steal credentials directly through AI coding tools.

For my financial advisor client, this isn't abstract. She handles data regulated by the SEC. A single breach doesn't just cost money — it can end her business. AI gave her a quick solution. But "quick" and "compliant" aren't the same thing.

And here's the kicker: **this is exactly how overpriced SaaS companies keep you locked in.** They know compliance is complicated. They know you're afraid of getting it wrong. So they charge premium prices for the privilege of not having to think about it — whether their solution actually fits your needs or not.
